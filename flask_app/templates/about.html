About PlatformPose

L. Archer Porter

Overview

PlatformPose is an open-source screen-capture pose estimation tool designed for researchers studying social media dance videos. It uses MediaPipe's Pose Landmarker model to extract body keypoint data from screen recordings, without requiring direct API access to the platforms on which videos are hosted.

The tool produces a structured corpus of pose landmarks — one row per accepted frame, across as many videos as a researcher captures — stored in a portable SQLite database and exportable as CSV for downstream movement analysis, visualization, or machine learning workflows.

Research Context

PlatformPose was developed in response to the progressive contraction of social media data access for academic researchers — what has been termed the post-API research landscape. Commercial platforms have increasingly restricted or eliminated research-grade data access, creating methodological challenges for scholars working with digital cultural materials at scale.

The tool addresses this constraint by operating at the screen level. Rather than retrieving video files or metadata through a platform API, it captures what is visible during normal playback in a browser. This approach is platform-agnostic: it works with any video that can be played in a browser window, regardless of where it is hosted.

How It Works

  1. Define a capture region. The researcher draws a rectangle over the video player using the region selector tool. Coordinates are saved and applied to subsequent captures.
  2. Launch the control panel. A small floating window appears on screen. The researcher navigates to the target video in another tab and begins playback.
  3. Capture. Pressing Start triggers a loop that screenshots the capture region at each iteration, runs MediaPipe pose detection on the frame, and records the 33 body landmarks if the pose is sufficiently visible.
  4. Filter. Frames where too many keypoints fall below the visibility threshold are discarded, reducing noise from occlusion or blur.
  5. Floor estimation. A floor reference position is computed from the ankle landmarks using either a rolling minimum or a foot-contact inference method.
  6. Corpus output. Accepted frames are appended to a project-level SQLite database, enabling comparison across videos from a single project.

Author

L. Archer Porter is a researcher working at the intersection of digital humanities, performance studies, and computational movement analysis. PlatformPose was developed as part of ongoing research into vernacular dance archives and the methodological challenges of studying ephemeral digital culture in a post-API era.

PlatformPose is open-source software. Source code and full documentation will be made publicly available upon publication.
